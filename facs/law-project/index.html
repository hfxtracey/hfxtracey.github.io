Dodd-Frank
Law

Law 111-203, enacted on July 21st, 2010

Overview of Law

After the recession of 2008, necessary economic reform was put into place. This law prevents companies from becoming too big and economically unstable. The law also gives the government the ability to shut down companies that they deem unsafe to the economy. Dodd-Frank also helps to insure large corporate loans in the case of bankrupcy and puts a regulation on those loans so insurance will actually be enough to cover the debt. One thing that will be prevented is banks using people's money to trade, similar to what caused the Great Depression in the early 20th century.

HISTORY

"​In the aftermath of the 2008 financial crisis, the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2010 (Dodd-Frank Act) enhanced the CFTC’s regulatory authority to oversee the more than $400 trillion swaps market." 

AMENDMENTS

There Have been 24 amendments to this law. Some of the major changes were to ensure non-bank financial instutions were covered, more protections of the American taxpayer, and to cover credit rating agencies. (Full list can be found here)
