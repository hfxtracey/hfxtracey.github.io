Veteran Scams

Steven Tracey

The goal of most Veteran Scams is to steal money by claiming to be government agencies and gathering sensitive information such as social security numbers or bank information.

Types of Veteran Scams

Phishing

  • Generally via email
  • Disguises to look legitimate
  • Generally pose as a government agency such as the Veterans Affairs (VA)

Identity Theft

  • Involves someone stealing personal information
  • Often centered around monetary gain
  • Easier to target vets because many documents contain sensitive info

Imposter Scam

  • Simlar to Phishing Scams
  • Pretend to be someone else
  • Generally more difficult to detect than phising
  • Sometimes disguised as a romantic interest

Warning Signs

  • Suspicious emails sent from public email domains (@gmail.com or @yahoo.com)
  • High pressure requests
  • Charging money for free records
  • Requiring upfront payment
  • Exclusive deals for veterans
  • Suspicious links

What to do

If you or someone you know is being targeted for a veteran scam, a report can be made to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), or the Federal Bureau of Investigation's Internet Crime Complaint Center.

How to avoid

  • Ensure all emails are from a trusted domain (@va.gov)
  • Check for spelling and grammar mistakes
  • Be mindful when clicking links contained in emails
  • Only enter sensitive details if the site is secured (https and lock icon)
