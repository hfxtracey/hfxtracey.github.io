7 Sources of Consumer Information

Consumer Protection Agencies
and Organizations

The BBB is a Consumer Protection Organization that is a leader in public services related to ethical business practices and dispute resolution.

Reliability

This Organization is very well known in the Consumer Protection industry. They have also successfully dealth with countless consumer complaints. These alongside other reasons make the BBB a very reliable source.

Would I Use It

I would use this source to make a real consumer decision because they have dealt with countless consumer complaints and generally know what they are talking about.

Professional Advisors

Edward Jones is a financial advisor company because they give resources to financial advisors as well as consumer related news, why people should come to them, and give consumers resources about financials.

Reliability

This company is widely used across the country and has many good reviews. Because of this, I think they are quite reliable.

Would I Use It

I would use this company for two main reasons. They have a very good reputation across the country, and they are close to home, giving them a sense of comfort to us.

Media Sources

CNN Underscored is an online shopping guide for the best in style, tech, health and travel. Their editors search the Web to find you the products and services they think consumers will love.

Reliability

This source is a little wishy washy due to on of CNN's subsidiary companies, CNN Politics which tend to be rather biased. Giving them the benefit of the doubt as they aren't very related to politcs, I would rate them 3.5/5 in reliability.

Would I Use It

I would most likely not use this source on its own because of the biases held by their sister subsidiaries. This could cause certain biases to carry over into this souce.

Package Information

This image is of a packaging label from a food item. These are regulated by the FDA in order to truthfully show the contents of the packaging.

Reliability

This is a very reliable source. This is because it is heavily regulated by the FDA and is illegal if it is wrong. I would rate this source a 5/5 in reliability.

Would I Use It

I would 100% use this source because it is so heavily regulated by the FDA.

Advertising

This is an advertisment for Coca Cola. This is stating that drinking their product will bring you happiness.

Reliability

This is not a very reliable source of information because it is put out by the company trying to sell their own product. I would rate advertisments a 1/5 in reliability.

Would I Use It

I would not rely on this soley because their company is the one putting the ad out and they have a bias towards themselves because they want to sell more product.

Salespeople

An example of salespeople are car salesmen. These people are trying to get you to buy a car so they get paid more.

Reliability

These people are somewhat reliable, they try to upsell you a vehicle because they are paid by commission, but they would be able to help you find which type of vehicle you need. I would rate them a 2.5/5 in overall reliability.

Would I Use It

I would use this source in order to determine what type of vehicle I am looking for, but not for which specific one because they are trying to get me to buy something from them in order to profit.

Other Consumers

Other consumers can be in the form of product reviews or talking to other people about a product.

Reliability

Depending on who you go to, other consumers can either be very reliable or not very reliable. Some people are not biased towards a particular brand whereas others will only use 1 brand. I would rate other consumers from a 1/5 to a 5/5 in reliability.

Would I Use It

I would generally use other consumers as a source of research because I would make sure I asked people who would give the honest truth about a product instead of ones who will defend brands no matter what.
