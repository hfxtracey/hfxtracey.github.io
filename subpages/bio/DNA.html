DNA

DNA Structure

  • DNA Stands for Deoxyribonucleic acid
  • DNA is a Nucleic Acid
    • Nucleic acids are made up of manysmaller units called nucleotides
    • Components of the DNA nucleotides
      • Deoxyribose (a simple sugar)
      • Phosphate group
      • Nitrogen base
  • DNA is in a form of a double helix (ladder)
    • Sides of the ladder
      • Sugar – deoxyribose
      • Acid – phosphate
    • Rungs of the ladder made up of Nitrogen Bases
      • Adenine – (A)
      • Thymine – (T)
      • Cytosine – (C)
      • Guanine – (G)
    • In DNA, hydrogen bonds form in the rungs as follows:
      • Cytosine bonds ONLY with Guanine C-G or G-C
      • Thymine always bonds with Adenine A-T or T-A
  • Watson and Crick developed the double Helix model of DNA
  • DNA is found in the nucleus of everycell in your body
  • Organisms are different because of the order of the nucleotides
  • The more closely related organismsare, the closer the order and length of the strands
